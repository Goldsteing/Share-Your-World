What is a Yawl?

A yawl is a type of two-masted sailing boat where the smaller rear mast—called the mizzen mast—is positioned behind the rudder post. The yawl's mizzen sail is typically used more for balance and steering rather than propulsion. Yawls were especially popular in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, commonly used for both racing and cruising.

  • Double mast
  • Uses three or more sails.
  • Doesn't need a rudder to easily steer
